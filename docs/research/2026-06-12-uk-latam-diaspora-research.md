# UK → Latin America diaspora & travel demand — verified research report

**Date:** 2026-06-12 · **Method:** deep-research workflow (5 search angles, 19 sources, 86 claims extracted, 25 verified by 3-vote adversarial panels → 21 confirmed, 4 refuted, synthesized to 9 findings)
**Purpose:** destination-guide ordering and expansion for fab.flights (Ecuador pinned first — core WhatsApp community).

## Executive summary

Census 2021 country-of-birth data ranks the Latin American diaspora: Brazil far first (~111k–116k), Colombia second (~38k–39k), then a near-tie between Venezuela (~20k–22k) and Ecuador (~20k), followed by Argentina, Mexico, Peru, Chile, Bolivia. Panama is so small it is not separately coded; all of Central America combined (23,573, including Mexico) is an order of magnitude below South America (254,222). QMUL's diaspora studies confirm the same ordering and show the census undercounts the real community by ~1.7×, disproportionately Ecuadorians and Bolivians. CAA data confirms Brazil and Colombia as the top direct-air markets, while Ecuador, Peru and Panama have zero direct UK service — their VFR demand flies via connecting hubs, which is precisely where a diaspora agency adds value. Venezuela is the clearest missing guide.

**Recommended order:** Ecuador (pinned) · Colombia · Brasil · Perú · Panamá — with Venezuela as the first new guide to add (slotting ahead of Panamá once written).

## Verified findings (all 3-0 adversarial votes unless noted)

1. **Census 2021 diaspora ranking (E&W, verifier re-queried the ONS flexible API, `country_of_birth_190a`):** Brazil 111,477 · Colombia 37,831 · Venezuela 19,980 · Ecuador 19,613 · Argentina 16,414 · Mexico 13,175 · Peru 10,943 · Chile 7,883 · Bolivia 7,403. UK-wide figures (Brazil 116,175; Colombia 38,968; Venezuela 21,826; Ecuador 19,980) preserve the same order.
2. **The Panama problem:** South America-born 254,222 vs Central America-born 23,573 (incl. Mexico). Panama is not separately coded anywhere in Census 2021 ("Other Central America", six countries combined, totals 9,205) — at most a few thousand UK residents. Weakest diaspora base of the current five destinations.
3. **Census undercount ~1.7× (QMUL "No Longer Invisible" 2011 / "Towards Visibility" 2016):** corrected estimates 113,500 London / 186,500 UK (2011) → ~145,000 London / ~250,000 UK (2013). Towards Visibility Table 2.6: UK 245,378 = 144,470 census + 46,002 second-gen + 13,383 NINo + 39,257 EU-passport + 2,266 irregular. Undercount disproportionately affects Ecuadorians and Bolivians — Ecuador's true community size likely exceeds Venezuela's despite the census near-tie.
4. **Ranking stable across 15 years:** Brazil > Colombia > {Ecuador, Argentina, Venezuela, Mexico} in 2011 Census, 2016 QMUL, and Census 2021. Caveat: Ecuador is **3rd in London** (7,171 in 2011) but only 5th nationally — Ecuador's strength is a London phenomenon, which matches fab.flights' positioning.
5. **Community character:** Colombians and Ecuadorians are the longest-established groups in London (deep VFR roots); Brazilians the largest, most recent and fastest-growing; post-2015 Venezuelan migration is the newest wave (~tripled to 20–22k by 2021).
6. **Direct air demand (CAA Table 12.1, 2023, verifiers extracted the official files):** Brazil 596,105 direct pax (LHR–GRU 496,215 + LHR–GIG 99,890, +23% YoY) = 63% of all direct UK–South America traffic; Colombia 165,827 (LHR–BOG, +32% YoY — the fastest-growing direct UK–LatAm route, beating Mexico City +30% and São Paulo +25%). March 2026 confirms the pattern (Brazil 53,316; Colombia 16,581 +29%). Also: Argentina 98,015; Chile 73,455 (2023).
7. **Three of the five current destinations are invisible in route data:** Ecuador and Panama have zero direct UK service; Peru recorded 181 token passengers in 2023 (BA's LGW–Lima never resumed post-2020). All VFR demand to these flies via Madrid/Amsterdam/Bogotá/Panama City — the agency's core value proposition.
8. **Missing guide candidates, priority order:** (1) **Venezuela** — 3rd-largest diaspora, fastest-growing, Spanish-speaking, connecting-hubs-only; (2) Argentina — 5th-largest (~16–18k) with a direct LHR–EZE route, though birth counts include non-diaspora British-family births; (3) Mexico — large direct traffic but overwhelmingly tourism; (4) Bolivia — small census count (7,403) but QMUL-flagged as heavily undercounted and adjacent to the Ecuadorian audience. Dominican Republic and Cuba are small.
9. **Recommended order (synthesis, medium confidence — editorial judgment over verified facts):** 1 Ecuador (pinned) · 2 Colombia (largest Spanish-speaking diaspora; longest-established; fastest-growing route; Bogotá is the Avianca hub for Ecuador itineraries) · 3 Brasil (largest diaspora and air market, but Portuguese-speaking — a distinct audience from the Spanish-first WhatsApp community) · 4 Perú · 5 Venezuela (new guide, when written) · 6 Panamá (keep; reframe partly as the Copa hub gateway). Colombia-vs-Brasil at slot 2 is the only contestable call — pure data says Brasil, audience affinity says Colombia.

## Refuted claims (do not cite)

- "ONS Travel Trends provides per-country visits broken down by VFR purpose" — **refuted 0-3**. Per-country UK→LatAm VFR volumes are NOT directly derivable from published Travel Trends spreadsheets; CAA totals mix business/tourism/VFR/connections.
- "Bolivia is a top-five LatAm community in London" — refuted 0-3 (Bolivia is 9th by census count).
- TS012 South America total of 249,639 from the v2 CSV — refuted (correct figure 254,222).
- "Ecuador, Peru, Panama, Venezuela and Bolivia have no rows in CAA March 2026" — refuted on technicalities; the verified 2023 version (finding 7) stands.

## Caveats

1. VFR-specific demand per country was not measurable — travel-demand findings rest on total direct passengers (mixed purpose; LHR–BOG includes onward connections to Quito/Lima).
2. Census country-of-birth undercounts the community ~1.7× and excludes the UK-born second generation; hits Ecuadorians/Bolivians hardest.
3. Most precise figures are England & Wales; UK-wide totals only partially verified (order preserved).
4. QMUL studies use 2011/2013 data; Venezuelan migration grew sharply afterwards.
5. Census data is March 2021 — five years old as of this report.
